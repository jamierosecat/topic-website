The Carboniferous Period: When Bugs Ruled the World

The Carboniferous Period was a great time to be an insect. With an overabundance of newly evolved plant-life, oxygen levels in the atmosphere were about 15% higher than they are today. (source) This allowed for insects, which take in oxygen through spiracles on their body, to grow to abundant sizes. Meganeura, a species of prehistoric dragonfly, had wingspans over two feet long, about the size of a small bird, while modern dragonflies have wingspans between 0.8 and 6 inches. (source)
